Embodied-AI meets digital-earth for Resiliency and Sustainability assurance in CONNected cities (EARS-CONN)

Call for Poster Presentations

EARS-CONN invites research that advances how embodied-AI — from ground robotics to satellite remote sensing — builds a living Digital-Earth for smarter city planning and more resilient urban infrastructure. Submit an abstract or short paper for peer-reviewed poster presentation at EARS-CONN 2026.

Scope

EARS-CONN 2026 is a long-vision initiative on Embodied-AI meets digital-earth for Resiliency and Sustainability assurance in CONNected cities (EARS-CONN). We seek poster presentations that address implementation plans — from embodied-AI agents to robots on the ground — on how they build, support, and protect Digital-Earth through cloud, connectivity, remote sensing, and AI/ML for resilient and sustainable urban ecosystems. A central theme is the application of Digital-Earth to urban insurance intelligence: satellite and aerial data provide the wide-area hazard picture, yet remote sensing alone cannot resolve the property-level detail that dense cities demand. Embodied-AI robotics are a critical complement, capturing roof conditions, structural integrity, micro-topography, and drainage behavior to power hyper-local risk models and property-specific digital twins for proactive underwriting and claims.

Topics of Interest

  • Improving NYC life: robotics and AI for urban quality of life, mobility, safety, sustainability
  • Implementation plans: Digital-Earth and embodied-AI for sustainability assurance
  • Digital-Earth: infrastructure, data fusion, Digital-Earth for urban and environmental applications
  • Data from satellites, robots, drones, autonomous vehicles, UAVs—building and maintaining Digital-Earth
  • Technical, regulation, or assurance perspectives for NYC and urban deployment
  • Urban and environmental sustainability and safety in the NYC area
  • Governance, standards, and monitoring for ethical decisions
  • Urban insurance & risk transfer: Digital-Earth for proactive hazard prediction, property-level risk scoring, digital twins for underwriting and claims, and parametric insurance triggers informed by satellite and robotic data
  • Remote sensing limitations & robotic complement: where satellite/aerial data falls short in urban environments and how ground robotics close the precision gap for insurance, infrastructure assessment, and emergency response
