About the Workshop

Advances in communication technologies—ranging from WiFi and 5G to satellite and emerging 6G architectures—enable robots to operate not only as standalone units but also as interconnected systems that share information, coordinate actions, and access distributed intelligence, with applications and decision-making increasingly leveraging connectivity to edge and cloud resources. This workshop focuses on how communication networks empower robotic autonomy and how autonomous robotic systems impose new requirements for communication infrastructures.

Call for Papers

We invite submissions presenting novel research, methodologies, and applications in networked robotic systems. All papers will be peer-reviewed for originality, relevance, technical quality, and clarity by our Technical Program Committee. Accepted papers will be presented as posters. At least one author must attend in person to present.

Topics of Interest

  • Communication-aware perception, planning, and control for autonomous robots
  • Network-constrained pose estimation, localization, and state estimation
  • Edge-cloud robotics and digital twins for real-time autonomous systems
  • Cloud-assisted learning, manipulation, and centralized perception under latency and reliability constraints
  • Network-enabled multi-robot and multi-agent coordination in autonomy-critical scenarios
  • Joint communication and sensing for robotic perception and control
  • Heterogeneous network support (WiFi, cellular, satellite) for connected autonomous systems
  • Communication requirements and architectures derived from robotic autonomy workloads
  • Network-aware safety and robustness mechanisms for connected autonomous robots
  • Evaluation, benchmarking, and experimental validation of network-constrained robotic systems

Timeline

  • Provisional Deadline for Paper submissions: April 17, 2026
  • Provisional Notification of acceptance: May 8, 2026

Format

  • Limited to 8 pages.
  • We welcome both original research contributions and selected previously published work that is highly relevant and of strong technical quality.
  • Presenting your work at the workshop will not interfere with its future publication elsewhere.
