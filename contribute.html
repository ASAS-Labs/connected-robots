Embodied-AI meets digital-earth for Resiliency and Sustainability assurance in CONNected cities (EARS-CONN)

Contribute to EARS-CONN 2026

EARS-CONN invites contributions — papers, talks, and challenge submissions — that advance how embodied-AI, from ground robotics to satellite remote sensing, builds a living Digital-Earth for smarter, more resilient cities. A key focus is on urban insurance intelligence: how Digital-Earth platforms enable proactive hazard prediction, property-specific risk assessment, and ethical automated claims — and why robotics is an essential complement to remote sensing in dense urban environments where satellite data alone cannot capture the ground-level detail insurers need. All submissions are peer reviewed. Choose how you want to contribute:

Abstract & Short Paper — Poster Presentation

Submit an abstract (1–2 pages) or short paper (2–4 pages) for poster presentation. Accepted submissions will be presented in the poster session during the 2-day event. Peer reviewed.

We welcome contributions aligned with EARS-CONN: embodied-AI, robotics, remote sensing, and Digital-Earth for resilient and sustainable connected cities — including applications in urban insurance and risk assessment, climate-change resilience, property-level digital twins, and the complementary role of ground robotics where satellite imagery falls short.

Submit Abstract / Short Paper

Digital-Earth Innovation Challenge

Submit innovative ideas for resilient and sustainable urban ecosystems through embodied-AI, robotics, and Digital-Earth. Peer reviewed. Possible awards and technology advancement sponsorships for the most impactful ideas.

How can embodied-AI — from agents to ground robots — build, support, and protect Digital-Earth for Resiliency and Sustainability assurance in CONNected cities? We are especially interested in ideas that leverage Digital-Earth for urban insurance: proactive hazard prediction, hyper-local risk scoring, property digital twins for underwriting and claims, and solutions that combine satellite remote sensing with robotic ground-truth data to overcome the precision limits of aerial observation alone.
