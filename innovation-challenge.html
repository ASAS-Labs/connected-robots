Embodied-AI meets digital-earth for Resiliency and Sustainability assurance in CONNected cities (EARS-CONN)

Digital-Earth Challenge

EARS-CONN challenges you to show how embodied-AI — from ground robots and drones to satellite remote sensing — builds a living Digital-Earth that enables smarter city planning and more resilient urban infrastructure. Peer reviewed. Possible awards and technology advancement sponsorships.

Challenge Focus

The Digital-Earth Challenge invites ideas aligned with EARS-CONN: Embodied-AI meets digital-earth for Resiliency and Sustainability assurance in CONNected cities. We seek innovative approaches that address resilient and sustainable urban ecosystems, and leverage data from satellites, embodied-AI systems, robots, drones, and sensors to build and keep Digital-Earth updated for real-time ethical decisions.

A priority focus is urban insurance intelligence: demonstrating how Digital-Earth platforms transform insurance from reactive to proactive — enabling precise hazard prediction, property-specific digital twins, hyper-local risk scoring, and ethical automated underwriting and claims. Critically, satellite and aerial remote sensing provides the macro-scale picture but cannot resolve the structural and micro-topographic detail that dense urban environments require; we particularly encourage ideas that integrate embodied-AI robotics as a complementary ground-truth layer to close this precision gap for insurers, city planners, and first responders.

Example Themes

  • Improving NYC life: robotics and AI for urban quality of life, mobility, safety, sustainability
  • Implementation plans: Digital-Earth and embodied-AI for sustainability assurance
  • Urban sustainability: smart cities, resource flow, infrastructure monitoring
  • Environmental sustainability: climate, biodiversity, pollution, natural resources
  • Safety: disaster response, risk assessment, ethical decision-making
  • Data fusion: combining satellite, robot, drone, and sensor data for Digital-Earth
  • Governance and assurance: standards, monitoring, and ethical deployment
  • Urban insurance & proactive risk: Digital-Earth for hyper-local hazard prediction (flood, wildfire, hail), property digital twins for underwriting, personalized premiums, and faster claims processing
  • Robotics as the insurance ground layer: autonomous robots and drones capturing roof conditions, façade integrity, vegetation proximity, and drainage micro-topography to fill the precision gap that satellite remote sensing cannot cover in dense cities
  • Climate-change resilience for insurers: defensible-space evaluation, long-term risk trajectory modeling, and parametric trigger design informed by combined satellite and robotic data streams

Resources & Infrastructure

Participants can leverage:

  • Satellite and Earth observation data
  • Cloud, connectivity, compute, and AI/ML infrastructure
  • Robotic platforms and embodied AI solutions
  • Open datasets and APIs for environmental and urban sensing

Submission & Pitch

Shortlisted teams will present their ideas during the event. A jury panel will evaluate proposals and announce winners for the most impactful Digital-Earth ideas.
