Implementation Plan: How to Improve Life in the NYC Area with Robotics and AI — Digital-Earth as Infrastructure

Digital-Earth Challenge

Innovation Ideas for NYC — Submit innovative ideas for improving life in the NYC area through Robotics, AI, and Digital-Earth as Infrastructure. Peer reviewed. Possible awards and technology advancement sponsorships. How can embodied-AI—from AI agents to ground robots—build, support, and protect Digital-Earth for urban and environmental sustainability and safety?

Challenge Focus

The Digital-Earth Challenge invites ideas aligned with our initiative: Implementation Plan for improving life in the NYC area with Robotics and AI — Digital-Earth as Infrastructure. We seek innovative approaches that address urban sustainability, environmental sustainability and safety, and leverage data from satellites, autonomous cars, robots, airplanes, drones, and UAVs to build and keep Digital-Earth updated for real-time ethical decisions.

Who Can Submit

We welcome challenge submissions from:

  • Undergraduate students
  • Graduate students
  • Research labs
  • Startup companies

Example Themes

  • Improving NYC life: robotics and AI for urban quality of life, mobility, safety, sustainability
  • Implementation plans: Digital-Earth as Infrastructure for NYC and urban areas
  • Urban sustainability: smart cities, resource flow, infrastructure monitoring
  • Environmental sustainability: climate, biodiversity, pollution, natural resources
  • Safety: disaster response, risk assessment, ethical decision-making
  • Data fusion: combining satellite, robot, drone, and sensor data for Digital-Earth
  • Governance and assurance: standards, monitoring, and ethical deployment

Resources & Infrastructure

Participants can leverage:

  • Satellite and Earth observation data
  • Cloud, connectivity, compute, and AI/ML infrastructure
  • Robotic platforms and embodied AI solutions
  • Open datasets and APIs for environmental and urban sensing

Submission & Pitch

Shortlisted teams will present their ideas during the event. A jury panel will evaluate proposals and announce winners for the most impactful Digital-Earth ideas.
