EARS-CONN 2026

Embodied AI for Resilient & Sustainable Systems
Connected to Earth and Space

New York City, USA • Dates TBA • 3–4 Days • Single-track talks + panels

Conference Vision

As autonomous robotic systems begin operating at large scale across cities, infrastructure, and environmental domains, EARS-CONN brings together experts from robotics and embodied AI, environmental science, remote sensing, satellite/CubeSat systems, policy & governance, and urban systems & sustainability.

Central question: How do we design safe, scalable, ethical, and environmentally responsible embodied AI systems for a planet where robots operate among humans and ecosystems?

Conference Format (Refined)

  • 3–4 days total
  • 2 days: talks + panels (single-track, shared audience)
  • 1–2 days: workshops + innovation challenge
  • 5–7 hosts / organizers (TBA)
  • 8–11 invited speakers (TBA)
  • 3 thematic sessions (sequential, not parallel)

Each theme includes 1–2 keynote talks to set direction and one moderated multidisciplinary panel to spark cross-disciplinary discussion.

Core Program Structure (Single-Track)

Thematic Block 1 — Technical Barriers to Scalable Embodied AI

Focus: Engineering and scientific challenges that limit safe, large-scale deployment of autonomous robotic systems.

Keynote topics (1–2 speakers):

  • Scalability challenges in embodied AI
  • Safety verification for city-scale robotics
  • Robust autonomy in unstructured environments
  • Satellite-connected robotic ecosystems

Moderated panel theme: “From Lab to Planet-Scale Deployment: What Is Still Missing?”

Panel composition (example): robotics safety researcher, AI systems engineer, remote sensing scientist, infrastructure/urban systems expert, industry representative.

Discussion points: reliability at scale, maintenance/lifecycle costs, energy sustainability, connectivity + remote sensing integration, infrastructure readiness.

Thematic Block 2 — Ethical AI Governance for Embodied Systems

Context: Embodied AI operates physically in human environments—raising safety, legal, and ethical questions.

Keynote topics (1–2 speakers):

  • Governance frameworks for embodied AI
  • Safety certification and public trust
  • Ethical risks in physical AI systems

Moderated panel theme: “Who Governs Robots in Public Spaces?”

Panel composition (example): AI ethics expert, policy researcher, robotics safety engineer, legal/regulatory specialist, public-sector representative.

Discussion points: accountability, safety standards/certification, transparency/explainability, societal acceptance, global coordination.

Thematic Block 3 — Embodied AI for Climate & Environmental Action

Focus: Using robotics and AI as active tools to address urgent environmental and sustainability challenges.

Keynote topics (1–2 speakers):

  • Robotics for ecosystem monitoring
  • Remote sensing + embodied AI integration
  • Climate-response robotic systems

Moderated panel theme: “Can Robots Help Heal the Planet?”

Panel composition (example): environmental scientist, climate systems researcher, remote sensing expert, robotics researcher, sustainability policy expert.

Discussion areas: ecosystem restoration, sustainable forest management, desertification, biodiversity monitoring, water resource management, robot + satellite sensing.

Workshops (1–2 Days)

Workshop 1 — Hands-On Safety Programming for Autonomous Robots

  • Program mobile robots
  • Implement safety rules, social norms, and human-aware navigation
  • Learn risk-aware motion planning and safe public-space operation

Workshop 2 — Remote Sensing + Satellite Data for Robotics

  • Using Earth observation data in robotics
  • Satellite-connected autonomy
  • CubeSat data pipelines for environmental robotics
  • Urban monitoring applications

NYC Innovation Challenge

Theme: Embodied AI + Remote Sensing for a Safe, Sustainable, and Friendly NYC

Focus areas:

  • Environmental monitoring robots
  • Disaster response systems
  • Smart infrastructure inspection
  • Sustainable delivery robotics
  • Air/water quality sensing networks

Structure: idea submission phase → shortlisted teams pitch live → jury panel evaluates.

Possible awards: Best Sustainability Impact, Best Safety Innovation, Best Urban Solution, Student Innovation Award.
